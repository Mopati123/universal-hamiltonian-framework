## I. The Mathematics of Faster-Than-Light

### 1.1 Special Relativity Revisited

**Einstein's energy-momentum relation**:
$$E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2$$

**For normal particles** ($v < c$):
- $m^2 > 0$ (real mass)
- $E$ real and positive
- Causal propagation

**For photons** ($v = c$):
- $m^2 = 0$ (massless)
- $E = pc$
- Light-speed propagation

**For tachyons** ($v > c$):
$$m^2 < 0 \quad \text{(imaginary mass!)}$$

Let $m^2 = -\mu^2$ where $\mu$ is real:
$$E^2 = (pc)^2 - (\mu c^2)^2$$

**Still real energy!** Just different dispersion relation.

### 1.2 The Tachyonic Dispersion Relation

**Standard particle**: $E = \sqrt{p^2c^2 + m^2c^4}$

**Tachyon**: $E = \sqrt{p^2c^2 - \mu^2c^4}$

**Velocity** (from $v = \frac{\partial E}{\partial p}$):
$$v = \frac{pc^2}{E} = c\sqrt{1 + \frac{\mu^2c^2}{p^2}}$$

**Key property**: As $p \to \infty$, $v \to c$ from above!

**Tachyons can't slow down to light speed** - the light barrier is a wall from both sides.

### 1.3 The Tachyonic Hamiltonian

**Standard Hamiltonian**:
$$H_{normal} = \frac{p^2}{2m} + V(q)$$

**Tachyonic Hamiltonian**:
$$H_{tachyon} = \frac{p^2}{2m} - V(q)$$

**THE CRUCIAL MINUS SIGN!**

**With $m^2 = -\mu^2$**:
$$H_{tachyon} = -\frac{p^2}{2\mu^2} - \frac{1}{2}\mu^2\omega^2 q^2$$

**Both kinetic AND potential inverted!**

### 1.4 Hamilton's Equations for Tachyons

$$\frac{dq}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p} = -\frac{p}{\mu^2}$$

$$\frac{dp}{dt} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q} = +\mu^2\omega^2 q$$

**Standard oscillator**: $\ddot{q} + \omega^2 q = 0$ (oscillation)

**Tachyonic oscillator**: $\ddot{q} - \omega^2 q = 0$ (exponential!)

**Solution**:
